Advection-Dispersion Equation

Background:

Advection is the process by which a contaminant is carried along with the velocity of the groundwater. Dispersion is the process by which contaminants spread away from the direct path of flow. These two processes combined can help solve contamination issues through the Advective-Dispersive Equation, or ADE. However, the ADE is idealized and does not always accurately represent what actually happens in these systems.
